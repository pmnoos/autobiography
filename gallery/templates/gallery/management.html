How Management Rights Work in Australia

with Ownership of a Manager’s Unit

The person or company managing the complex usually owns an apartment in the building. This unit is typically included in the purchase of the management rights. A Caretaking Agreement The manager is responsible for the upkeep and maintenance of common areas (gardens, pools, hallways, etc.). This agreement is signed with the body corporate (owners’ committee) and provides a guaranteed income. Letting Rights (Rental Management Business) The manager acts as a rental agent for property owners in the complex. They manage rentals, find tenants, handle bookings (for holiday apartments), and receive commission-based income. This is where the main profit comes from, especially in tourist areas. Financing and Buying Management Rights Buying management rights is like purchasing a business—a bank loan is often required. Agreements usually last 5-25 years, depending on state laws (Queensland allows longer agreements than other states). Types of Management Rights Permanent – Managing long-term rental apartments. Holiday Letting – Managing short-term rentals for tourists. Corporate Letting – Catering to business travelers. Mixed Use – A combination of the above.
